Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide pasta sheets with rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta, and melted cheese. Baked until bubbly and golden on top, it’s known for its hearty texture and comforting, savory flavors.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef (or ground sausage)
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Salt or black pepper
  • Dried oregano or Italian seasoning
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Egg (optional, for firmer layers)
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
    • Optional additions:
  • Fresh basil or parsely
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Spinach or mushrooms

Steps

  1. Cook the noodles

    2. Boil the lasagna noodles in salted water according to the package directions. Drain and lay them flat so they don’t stick.

  2. Make the meat sauce

    3. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until soft. Add garlic and cook briefly. Stir in the ground beef and cook until browned. Drain excess fat if needed. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, salt, pepper, and oregano or Italian seasoning. Simmer for 15–20 minutes.

  3. Prepare the cheese mixture

    4. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with the egg (if using), a little salt and pepper, and some grated Parmesan.

  4. Assemble the lasagna

    5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, then ricotta mixture, then meat sauce, then mozzarella. Repeat the layers, ending with sauce and mozzarella on top.

  5. Bake

    6. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake another 15–20 minutes, until the cheese is melted and lightly golden.

  6. Rest and serve

    7. Let the lasagna rest for about 10 minutes before slicing. This helps it hold its shape when served.
